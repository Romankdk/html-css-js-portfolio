    About Me

    I am a dedicated Cybersecurity student currently pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science & Information Security at John Jay College of Criminal Justice. With a strong foundation in computer science principles and a keen interest in protecting digital assets, I am passionate about understanding the complexities of cybersecurity and the importance of safeguarding information in our increasingly digital world.

    My academic journey began at LaGuardia Community College, where I earned my Associate of Science in Computer Science. Throughout my studies, I have developed proficiency in various programming languages, including Python, Java, and C++, as well as essential cybersecurity tools and practices. I am particularly interested in areas such as ethical hacking, network security, and cryptography.

    In addition to my coursework, I have gained practical experience as a Help Desk Technician at Norwich University, where I provided technical support to students and faculty, troubleshooting complex issues and ensuring the security of IT systems. I also worked as a Graphic Designer at VICE Software Company, where I created compelling visual identities and marketing materials.

    I am constantly seeking opportunities to expand my knowledge and skills in cybersecurity. I enjoy working on personal projects, such as developing a Password Manager with Encryption and a Train and Bus Ticket Management System, which have allowed me to apply my learning in real-world scenarios. Outside of my academic pursuits, I am passionate about staying updated with the latest trends and developments in cybersecurity, believing that continuous learning is essential in this ever-evolving field.
